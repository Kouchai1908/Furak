Our Culture

More Than Just Food,
It's a Connection.

In Timor-Leste, dining is a communal act that strengthens bonds. Sharing a meal like Tukir or Batar Da'an is about celebrating togetherness, honoring guests, and preserving the stories of our ancestors through taste.

Saboko

Saboko is a traditional Timorese fish dish cooked using a unique method with palm leaves. Fresh fish is marinated with local spices such as tamarind, turmeric, and chili, then wrapped tightly in palm leaves and grilled over charcoal. This cooking process gives the fish a distinctive aroma and deep flavor.

Cooking Time

25 minutes

Difficulty Level

Medium

Main Ingredients:

  • • Fresh fish (snapper/grouper)
  • • Young palm leaves
  • • Tamarind
  • • Turmeric and traditional spices
  • • Chili and onions

Saboko is not just food, but also a symbol of culinary tradition passed down through generations in Timor-Leste.

Tukir

Tukir is a very special ceremonial dish in Timorese culture. Meat that has been marinated with traditional spices is slowly cooked inside bamboo stalks. This unique cooking process gives a distinctive taste and appetizing aroma. Tukir is usually served at important events and traditional celebrations.

Cooking Time

4 hours

Difficulty Level

Hard

Main Ingredients:

  • • Beef or pork
  • • Fresh bamboo stalks
  • • Traditional Timorese spices
  • • Pandan leaves
  • • Coconut milk

Tukir symbolizes unity and togetherness in Timorese culinary tradition.

Batar Da'an

Batar Da'an is a traditional Timorese dish made from corn, mung beans, and pumpkin. This dish is a very nutritious and filling staple food. Cooked with coconut milk and local spices, Batar Da'an has a savory taste and soft texture. This dish is often served as daily food and also at special events.

Cooking Time

45 minutes

Difficulty Level

Easy

Main Ingredients:

  • • Corn kernels
  • • Mung beans
  • • Yellow pumpkin
  • • Coconut milk
  • • Salt and spices

Batar Da'an is a symbol of prosperity and abundance in Timorese culture.

Ikan Pepes

Ikan Pepes is a fish dish seasoned with distinctive Timorese spices, then wrapped tightly in banana leaves and steamed. This cooking process locks all the aroma and flavor of the spices into the fish meat, resulting in a very delicious and fragrant dish. Banana leaves give a distinctive natural aroma to this dish.

Cooking Time

30 minutes

Difficulty Level

Medium

Main Ingredients:

  • • Fresh fish (tilapia/carp)
  • • Banana leaves
  • • Traditional pepes spices
  • • Chili and onions
  • • Aromatic spices

Ikan Pepes represents local wisdom in processing seafood in a healthy and delicious way.

Kulu

Kulu is a traditional Timorese dessert made from breadfruit cooked with coconut milk. This sweet dish has a soft texture and creamy taste. Kulu is often served as a snack or dessert at family gatherings and traditional celebrations. Its naturally sweet taste from breadfruit and coconut milk makes it a favorite for all ages.

Cooking Time

15 minutes

Difficulty Level

Easy

Main Ingredients:

  • • Ripe breadfruit
  • • Thick coconut milk
  • • Palm sugar
  • • Salt to taste
  • • Pandan leaves

Kulu is a symbol of tenderness and warmth in Timorese culinary tradition.

Midar Sin

Midar Sin is a distinctive Timorese sweet and sour pork dish that uses local fruits to provide a fresh sour taste. The pork is cut into small pieces and cooked with traditional spices, then tropical fruits are added to give a unique flavor. This dish is popular at celebrations and is a favorite among locals.

Cooking Time

35 minutes

Difficulty Level

Medium

Main Ingredients:

  • • Fresh pork
  • • Local tropical fruits
  • • Sweet and sour seasoning
  • • Chili and onions
  • • Traditional spices

Midar Sin reflects Timorese culinary creativity in combining sweet and sour flavors.

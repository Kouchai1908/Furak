A Legacy of Resilience

Timor-Leste's story is one of endurance. From the arrival of Portuguese explorers to the rugged resistance in the mountains, the spirit of the people remains unbroken like the Kaebauk—a symbol of power and authority that curves like the crescent moon.

Traditional Tais weaving carries the stories of generations.

1515 — 1975

The Portuguese Era

Portuguese explorers arrived in the 16th century, seeking sandalwood. For over 400 years, their influence shaped the architecture, religion, and the introduction of coffee cultivation which remains a vital part of the economy today.

The Struggle & Resistance

star
"To resist is to win."

Following the Carnation Revolution in Portugal, Timor-Leste declared independence, only to be occupied shortly after. The Falintil resistance forces retreated to the mountains, beginning a 24-year struggle for sovereignty.

A New Dawn

Timor-Leste became the first new sovereign state of the 21st century. Today, the nation builds its future while honoring its past, focusing on reconciliation, development, and sharing its unique culture with the world.

Did you know?
"Timor" comes from the Malay word for "East", and "Leste" is the Portuguese word for "East". The country's name literally means "East-East".
